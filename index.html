Asciidoc-table-plugin

  • All tables must start three colons (:::) after an empty line.
  • All tables must end with three colons (:::).
  • The beginning of every cell is marked by a pipe (|).
  • An empty line is used to indicate the end of the header row.

For example,

:::
| Name | Country | Population

| Bengaluru | India | 14 MM
| Chennai | India | 12 MM
| Tokyo | Japan | 14 MM
| Berlin | Germany | 4 MM
:::

Multi-line

Table cell contents can span multiple lines. You can include lists inside a table cell also. 

:::
| Multi-line 
table demo
| Cell spans
multiple lines

|
You can multiple paragraphs inside a cell. This is 
useful when you have a lot of content.

If you are looking at the source (before rendering), it
does not look like a table. This scheme goes against 
the traditional design principles of common Markdown, 
which prefers to have readable source code. 

But common Markdown does not support multiple lines in a 
table cell, and that is quite a useful feature to have.

| You can have:
- Lists (numbered or not)
- ```Block quotes```
- and other elements

Inside a table cell.
:::

Cell wrapping

You don't have to manually format the table.

Cells will automatically wrap based on the number of columns in the first row.

So, you can do this:

:::
| Name
| Country
| Population

| Bengaluru
| India
| 14 MM

| Chennai
| India
| 12 MM

| Tokyo
| Japan
| 14 MM

| Berlin
| Germany
| 4 MM
:::

Alignment markers

You can align the contents of the table by using alignment markers.

:::
| Alignment | Marker

| Left | :---
| Right | ---:
| Center | :---:
:::

For example,

:::
| :--: | :--: | --:
| Name | Country | Population

| Bengaluru | India | 14 MM
| Chennai | India | 12 MM
| Tokyo | Japan | 14 MM
| Berlin | Germany | 4 MM
:::

The minimum number of hyphens is 1, but you can include more if you want to align with the next row, like this: 

:::
| :--- | :-----: | ---------:
| Name | Country | Population


| Bengaluru | India | 14 MM
| Chennai | India | 12 MM
| Tokyo | Japan | 14 MM
| Berlin | Germany | 4 MM
:::
